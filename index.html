About

Hi! I am a fifth-year graduate student at the Department of Physics and Astronomy, Seoul National University where I'm currently pursuing a Ph.D. in astronomy under the supervision of professor Woong-Tae Kim in the Computational & Theoretical Astrophysics Group (CnTAG).

Research

With great help from my collaborators, I'm trying to understand how interstellar medium in galactic centers dynamically evolves, forms stars, launches galactic wind, and fuels nuclear activity.
  • Sanghyuk Moon, Woong-Tae Kim, Chang-Goo Kim, & Eve C. Ostriker 2021, Star Formation in Nuclear Rings with the TIGRESS Framework, ApJ, 914, 9
  • Sanghyuk Moon, Woong-Tae Kim, & Eve C. Ostriker 2019, A Fast Poisson Solver of Second-order Accuracy for Isolated Systems in Three-dimensional Cartesian and Cylindrical Coordinates, ApJS, 241, 24
  • Woong-Tae Kim, Sanghyuk Moon 2016, Equilibrium Sequences and Gravitational Instability of Rotating Isothermal Rings, ApJ, 829, 45
